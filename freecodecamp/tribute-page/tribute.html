Albert Einstein

The father of modern physics

A brief history of Einstein's life

  • 1879 - Born in Ulm, German Empire.

  • 1895 - Moved to Switzerland, forsaking his German citizenship.

  • 1897 - Enrolled in the mathematics and physics teaching diploma program at the Swiss Federal polytechnic school in ZÃ¼rich.

  • 1900 - Graduated.

  • 1901 - Acquired Swiss citizenship, which he kept for the rest of his life.

  • 1903 - Secured a permanent position at the Swiss Patent Office in Bern.

  • 1905 - Was awarded a PhD by the University of Zurich.

  • 1914 - Moved to Berlin in order to join the Prussian Academy of Sciences and the Humboldt University of Berlin.

  • 1917 - Became director of the Kaiser Wilhelm Institute for Physics; he also became a German citizen again, this time Prussian.

  • 1920 - Barnard Medal.

  • 1921 - Nobel Prize in Physics, Matteucci Medal.

  • 1933 - Adolf Hitler came to power in Germany. Einstein, as a Jew, lost his position. Was included in a list of enemies of the German regime with the phrase, "not yet hanged", offering a $5,000 bounty on his head.

  • 1940 - Settled in the United States and became an American citizen. Edorsed a letter to President Franklin D. Roosevelt alerting him about the danger of the German nuclear weapons program and recommending that the US begin similar research.

  • 1942 - Joins the National Academy of Sciences.

  • 1955 - Dies at the age of 76.
